Meet our team

The team is composed by architects and computer science engineers,
both practitioners and students in the referred specialities

Senior Researcher

António Menezes Leitão

António Menezes Leitão has a BSc in Mechanical Engineering, a MSc in Electronics Engineering, and a PhD in Computer Science and Engineering, all from Instituto Superior Técnico (IST) of the University of Lisbon. Currently he is Assistant Professor at the same university, senior researcher at INESC-ID, within the Graphics and Interaction Group, and Coordinator of ADA, teaching, lecturing, and researching on bringing together the fields of Computer Science and Architecture.
antonio.menezes.leitao@tecnico.ulisboa.pt

Junior Researchers

Gonçalo Araújo

Gonçalo Araújo is a PhD student of Sustainable Energy Systems at IST. He has a Master's in Architecture from IST, and has also studied and worked in the Architecture and Urban Planning department of Tongji University (Shanghai, China). Currently, he is also a junior researcher at IN+, researching representation, simulation, and optimization methods for more sustainable cities.
goncalo.r.araujo@tecnico.ulisboa.pt | Portfolio


Inês Caetano

Inês Caetano is an architect graduated at IST. She has also studied at Politecnico de Milano (Italy) during her master's. She is currently a junior researcher at INESC-ID, and an Architecture PhD student at IST, exploring the integration of algorithmic design methods in the design, analysis and optimization of facade designs.
ines.caetano@tecnico.ulisboa.pt | Portfolio


Inês Pereira

Inês Pereira is a Master's graduate in architecture from IST and an intern at INESC-ID. She is currently currently finishing a second MSc in Engineering and Data Science at IST while researching optimization processes and performance-based approaches to architectural design.
ines.pereira@tecnico.ulisboa.pt


Renata Castelo-Branco

Renata Castelo-Branco is a Master's graduate in architecture from IST, a junior researcher at INESC-ID, and a Computer Science and Engineering PhD student at the same university. She has also studied at the Technical University of Graz (Austria) during her master's. She is currently researching representation methods for algorithmic design.
renata.castelo.branco@tecnico.ulisboa.pt | Portfolio
